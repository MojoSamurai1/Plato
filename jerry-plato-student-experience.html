The Six Differentiators

1. Adaptive Recommendations

Plato learns what Mia struggles with and nudges her toward those topics â€” gently, not aggressively. "Statistics hasn't had a session in 5 days â€” fancy a quick 20 minutes?"

2. Spaced Repetition for Exam Prep

Based on the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, Plato schedules review sessions at optimal intervals. Topics she rated "struggling" come back sooner and more often.

3. Confidence Tracking (Not Grades)

Plato tracks how Mia feels about her learning, not just what grades she gets. This catches issues early â€” a student can pass but still feel lost, or struggle but be making real progress.

4. Encouragement & Insight Stories

After enough data, Plato generates warm observations: "Did you notice you study best in the morning? Your confidence ratings are highest before noon." Never preachy, always curious.

5. Study Habit Optimisation

Over time, Plato learns when Mia studies best, how long her focus lasts, which subjects she avoids, and gently helps her build better habits.

6. Assignment Decomposition

AI breaks complex assignments into step-by-step checklists with suggested timelines. Turns "write a 3000-word case study" into 10 manageable tasks.
